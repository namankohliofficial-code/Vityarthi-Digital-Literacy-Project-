-General online safety

1)Use strong, unique passwords and enable two‑factor authentication on email, social media, and banking apps.

2)Do not click on links from unknown SMS, emails, or WhatsApp messages claiming rewards, KYC failure, or lottery wins.

3)Install apps only from official app stores and check reviews/permissions before installing.

4)Avoid sharing personal data like Aadhaar number, full address, or college ID on public platforms.

5)Log out of accounts when using shared or lab computers and clear browser history.

-Financial / UPI‑specific safety

Remember: UPI PIN is only for sending money, never for receiving money. If someone asks you to enter PIN to get a refund or prize, it is a scam.

1)Double‑check the receiver’s UPI ID, name, and amount before approving a payment.

2)Never share OTPs, UPI PIN, CVV, or card details on calls or chats, even if the caller claims to be from bank or customer care.

3)If you suspect fraud, immediately block your UPI/card via bank app and report on cybercrime.gov.in and helpline 1930.
